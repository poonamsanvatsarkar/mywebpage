About Red Fort

The Red Fort is a historic fort in India that served as the residence of Mughal emperors. It is famous for its strong red sandstone walls and grand design.

It is located in Delhi and was built by Emperor Shah Jahan in 1648. The fort became the political and cultural center of the Mughal Empire.

The Red Fort is an important symbol of India’s heritage. Every year on Independence Day, the national flag is hoisted here.

It is also a UNESCO World Heritage Site and attracts many visitors from across the world.

Interesting Facts

  • The fort is made using red sandstone.
  • It took about 10 years to complete.
  • It was once home to Mughal emperors.
  • Independence Day celebrations happen here every year.
